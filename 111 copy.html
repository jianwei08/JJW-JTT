Frequently Asked Questions

When will the mine reopen?
We are liaising with Tasmanian Government departments on the requirements to reactivate underground mining and gold processing. On the current indicative schedule, it is expected that the mine may return to gold production by the end of 2026.
Why is the mine reopening?
With increasing gold demand and advances in modern mining technology, redeveloping the mine offers strong economic viability. We will also utilize more environmentally friendly extraction methods.
Why did the mine shut down?
[Insert historical context here, e.g., Operations were previously suspended due to technical limitations and market price fluctuations at the time.]
How will the community be involved?
We will hold regular community hearings and information-sharing sessions to ensure local residents stay informed on project progress, establishing dedicated channels to collect your feedback and suggestions.
What jobs will be available?
We will offer a wide variety of roles spanning geological exploration, heavy machinery operation, environmental monitoring, and logistics management, with a strong priority on hiring locally.
Will the rehabilitation of the wetlands be completed?
Yes, environmental protection is a core pillar of our restart plan. We pledge not only to complete the wetland rehabilitation but to implement ecological protection measures that exceed all previous standards.
